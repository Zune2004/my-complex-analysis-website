Historical Development in Complex Analysis

Complex analysis, also known as the theory of functions of a complex variable, is a branch of mathematics that studies complex numbers and functions of a complex variable.

The historical development of complex analysis can be traced back to the 18th century when mathematicians like Leonhard Euler and Jean le Rond d'Alembert started working on the foundations of complex numbers and their properties.

In the 19th century, Augustin-Louis Cauchy made significant contributions to complex analysis by introducing the concept of a complex function's limit, continuity, and the Cauchy-Riemann equations.
